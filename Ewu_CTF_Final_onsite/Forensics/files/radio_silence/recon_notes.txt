SIGINT INTERCEPT #4471 — Analyst Working Notes (partial)

Picked this transmission up on a monitored frequency last night.
Recording is ~29 seconds, mono, 44.1kHz. To the ear it's just static --
no voice, no obvious modulation you'd recognize by listening.

Ran it through a spectrum analyzer out of habit and there it is: this
isn't noise, or at least not ALL noise. There's a sync/marker tone at
the very start of the actual transmission (before that it really is
just empty channel noise) -- looks like it's there to mark "data
begins now" for whatever's on the receiving end. I make it out to be
sitting right around 800 Hz, sustained for a good while before
anything else happens.

After the marker ends, the signal changes character -- starts hopping
between what looks like exactly two discrete frequencies, in a long
sequence, sounds like classic binary FSK to me (frequency-shift
keying -- two tones, one for each bit value, hold each for a fixed
duration, that's your bitstream). Eyeballing the spectrogram the two
data tones look like they're sitting around 1.2 kHz and 2.4 kHz, give
or take -- exact values worth confirming yourself before you trust my
numbers off a rough visual read.

Didn't have time to work out the exact symbol duration (how long each
tone lasts before the next one) that's the next thing to nail down,
since decoding depends entirely on getting your window size right. Get
that wrong and you'll either skip bits or read the same bit twice.

If this really is FSK carrying a bitstream, packing it 8 bits to a
byte should turn it into readable text haven't gotten that far
yet. Didn't determine bit order (MSB or LSB first) either. Standard
convention is MSB-first for most protocols I've seen, that's my
starting guess.
